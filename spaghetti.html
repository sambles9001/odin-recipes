Title

Best Spaghetti

Description

This recipe is a classic. Homemade spaghetti made in the most traditional sense using dry pasta and fresh tomatoes. We use dry pasta for the al dente experience, meaning it will have a different texture than fresh pasta. Simplicity in its best form, this recipe takes pasta to the pinnacle of what it means to eat Italian.

Ingredients

  • dry spaghetti
  • olive oil
  • canned san marzano tomatoes
  • garlic
  • fresh basil
  • parmigiano reggiano
  • salt

Time to make Spaghetti

  1. Prep your ingredients by crushing the tomatoes, peeling your garlic and slicing it into small but easily removable slices, and removing the leaves from the basil stems but keep the stems
  2. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and add a heaping of salt and then add dry pasta
  3. While your pasta is cooking, add olive oil to a pan and bring the heat up to low and add garlic and basil stems
  4. Once the garlic is browning slightly you should add the crushed tomatoes into the pan and let that slowly cook until your pasta is almost ready to be added
  5. You will finish cooking your pasta in the pan with the sauce so do not overcook the pasta in the water. Remove the garlic and tomato stems from the sauce and then add the pasta and begin to move the pasta around to prevent overcooking areas
  6. Add a bit of pasta water to the pan as it adds starch to the dish and allows the sauce to thicken and stick to the pasta. If overly watery continue cooking the pasta down and if the sauce seems to be lacking body add a bit more pasta water
  7. Your pasta is done and you can place the dish and add basil leaves to garnish as well as grating your cheese on top!
